Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our warranty packages.

What is covered under your warranties?

Our warranties cover a wide range of mechanical and electrical components depending on the level of coverage you choose. The Standard plan covers essential systems like the engine and transmission, while our Gold and Platinum plans offer progressively more comprehensive coverage including electronics, air conditioning, and luxury features. Full details of coverage are provided in the warranty documentation.

Can I transfer my warranty if I sell my vehicle?

Yes, our warranties are transferable to a new owner, which can increase the resale value of your vehicle. There is a small administration fee for transferring the warranty. Please contact our warranty department for the current fee and to arrange the transfer.

How do I make a warranty claim?

Making a claim is simple. If you experience a fault, simply contact our claims department before any work is carried out. Our team will guide you through the process, authorize repairs with an approved repairer, and arrange payment directly to them. In some cases, you may need to pay a portion of the repair cost as per your warranty terms.

Are there any exclusions to the warranty coverage?

Yes, certain items are excluded from coverage as they are considered wear and tear items. These typically include (but are not limited to) battery, clutch friction material, brake pads and discs, tires, wiper blades, and routine service items. Additionally, damage from external causes such as accidents, improper fuel, or neglecting servicing requirements may void warranty coverage. Full details of exclusions are provided in your warranty documentation.

Do I need to service my vehicle at Better Performance to maintain warranty?

No, you can maintain your vehicle at any VAT-registered garage, provided they use genuine or OEM parts and follow the manufacturer's service schedule. You must keep all service records and receipts as proof of maintenance, as these will be required in the event of a claim.
